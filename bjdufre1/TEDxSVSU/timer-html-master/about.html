What is TEDxSVSU?

TEDxSVSU is an independently organized TED-style event, a full day of local, national and international speakers, music and artistic presenters, and also sponsors and exhibitors who showcase novel displays and activities. (See: Official website.) The target audience is SVSU and the surrounding community, especially students, parents, families, staff, faculty, and administration. The event attracts educated people throughout mid-Michigan and beyond. Anyone interested in TED-style talks is welcome to attend TEDxSVSU. Organizers and leaders of the event include SVSU students, faculty and staff. The project is supported by the SVSU Foundation Research Grant Program.

What is TEDx?

TEDx was created in the spirit of TED's mission, "ideas worth spreading." It supports independent organizers who want to create a TED-like event in their own community.






What is TED?

TED is a nonprofit devoted to spreading ideas, usually in the form of short, powerful talks (18 minutes or less). TED began in 1984 as a conference where Technology, Entertainment and Design converged, and today covers almost all topics — from science to business to global issues — in more than 100 languages. Meanwhile, independently run TEDx events help share ideas in communities around the world.

Where at?

TEDxSVSU will be held at Sagianw Valley State University in Curtis Hall. The conference will be in the Curtis Hall Banquet Room on the second floor. It can be reached be stairs or elevator located by the SVSU Bookstore. Signs will be posted at entryways.
